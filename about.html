About Me

Kendra Hettig is a recent graduate from California State University, Long Beach, where she received a Bachelor of Arts in Communication Studies and a minor in Public Relations. Her professional background includes marketing and public relations internships with MemorialCare Health System and the Long Beach Branch NAACP.

During Kendra’s marketing and public relations internship with MemorialCare Health System, she performed various marketing functions—including rebranding collateral, social media content development, copyediting, media pitching, campaign planning and story formation. As an intern, she was able to devise and implement a campaign aimed at educating patients and the local community about sleep health. Kendra utilized various mediums to reach target audiences, including e-newsletters, patient success stories, social media and podcasts featuring healthcare professionals.

Kendra’s previous experience also includes a communications internship with the Long Beach Branch NAACP. She learned the basic principles of creating captivating stories that promote a mission and worked alongside community leaders from diverse backgrounds to learn what it means to effectively spark change. During the course of her internship, she produced a variety of communications materials, garnered regional media placements, grew membership, assisted with event planning and attained hundreds of new followers across all social media channels.

Relevant coursework Kendra completed from CSULB includes Public Relations Case Studies, Written Communication for Public Relations and Digital Public Relations Toolbox. Throughout these courses, she learned essential knowledge on SEO, social media analytics and algorithms, content development and campaign strategy. She demonstrated her public relations knowledge through course projects—including a comprehensive social media proposal designed for the Women’s Shelter of Long Beach.

Kendra hopes to continue her passion for community relations, campaign strategizing and content development at her future marketing and public relations endeavors.
